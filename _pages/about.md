---Hello!

I am a researcher at the Institute for Health Policy Studies ([IEPS](https://ieps.org.br/institucional/#ancora-quemsomos)). I hold a PhD in Public Administration from the São Paulo School of Business Administration at Fundação Getulio Vargas ([FGV-EAESP](https://eaesp.fgv.br/en/about-eaesp)).

I serve as a Co-Principal Investigator (Co-PI) for an NIHR grant focused on racial health inequalities in Brazil. My work sits at the intersection of health economics and public health, covering topics such as decomposition analysis, health policy impact evaluations, affirmative action (the Quotas Law), and implicit bias.

In addition to my work on racial inequalities, my doctoral research explored the intersection of health and environmental economics in the Brazilian Amazon. Specifically, I investigated the effects of river seasonality on health outcomes, with a focus on transportation challenges and healthcare access as primary mechanisms.

Agenda: health economics, policy evaluation, and environmental economics.
